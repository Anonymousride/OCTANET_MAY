Jaguar C-X75

The Jaguar C-X75 is a high-performance hybrid-electric concept car that first debuted at the 2010 Paris Motor Show. It was initially created to celebrate Jaguar's 75th anniversary and showcase the brand's innovation and design prowess. The "C-X" in its name stands for "concept experimental," while "75" refers to Jaguar's milestone.
One of the most striking features of the C-X75 is its advanced powertrain. Originally, it was designed to be a range-extended electric vehicle (REEV), meaning it could run purely on electric power with its batteries, but it also had two micro gas turbines to generate electricity, extending its range significantly. This unique setup gave the C-X75 impressive performance figures, with a reported top speed of over 200 mph (320 km/h) and acceleration from 0 to 60 mph (0 to 97 km/h) in less than three seconds.
While the C-X75 was initially intended for production, Jaguar ultimately decided not to pursue mass production due to economic uncertainties at the time. However, the concept car gained further attention when it was featured in the James Bond film "Spectre" (2015), where it was driven by the villain, played by actor Dave Bautista, in a memorable chase scene.
